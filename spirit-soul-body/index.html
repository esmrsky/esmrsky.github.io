A word on method

Does science explain God away?

No — it explains the mechanism, not the meaning. A scan can show what happens in you when you pray or forgive. It can't tell you whether God is on the other end. Science and Scripture answer different questions.

Science asks

How?

Cortisol, neurons, networks — the mechanism. What happens, and how it works.

Scripture answers

Who & why?

Who made it, and for what — the meaning a mechanism can never hold.

Knowing how the watch works has never disproved the watchmaker. The clearer the design, the more there is to marvel at — the mechanism points to a Maker.

"Clearly seen… from what has been made" · Romans 1:20 · NIV

The two books

Two books, one Author

A historic Christian conviction: God has written two books — the book of his Word, and the book of his works. The same Author wrote both, so they cannot finally contradict.

Studying the brain, then, isn't a threat to faith. Done humbly, it's reading the second book — "thinking God's thoughts after him."

Sleep & movement as worship

The body runs on a design — a daily and weekly rhythm of work, rest and sleep. Honoring it is stewardship, not vanity.

Renewing the mind, literally

"Do not conform to the pattern of this world, but be transformed by the renewing of your mind."

Romans 12:2 · NIV

The adult brain isn't fixed — it's plastic. Every repeated thought strengthens a neural pathway. The brain rewires around what we dwell on.

Renewal isn't only a feeling. What you rehearse, you become.

A thought, once — a faint trace
Repeated — the pathway thickens
Rehearsed — a strong, automatic path

London cabbies who memorize 25,000 streets grow larger hippocampi.

Spirit · Intuition

Gut feelings & the listening heart

Scripture locates discernment in the heart — and even the gut. Paul's word for compassion, splagchna, literally means the bowels.

The body agrees. The gut and heart carry their own neural networks; most of the vagus nerve runs upward, body to brain.

Enteric nervous system · GershonHeart coherence · HeartMath

A gut feeling is real data — but data, not a verdict. A signal to weigh, not a voice to obey.
