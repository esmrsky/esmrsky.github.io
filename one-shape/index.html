the pattern · the structural why

One shape, many wells

A real and legitimate desire gets routed to a false source — and the falseness of the source is exactly what makes it loop.

Augustine called it disordered loves: the trouble usually isn't wanting something evil, it's wanting something good in the wrong order, or from the wrong place. Most of the suffering here comes from blaming the desire — "I'm weak," "more discipline" — instead of the sourcing.

"They have committed two evils: they have forsaken the fountain of living water, and hewed out cisterns for themselves — broken cisterns that can hold no water."Jeremiah 2:13
leak finder

Trace the thirst under the leak

A useful diagnosis doesn't start with "what is wrong with me?" It starts with: what good need keeps getting sent to a source that cannot hold it? Four questions trace the leak.

01 · The surface leak

The behaviour you'd name if asked — the scroll, the snack, the spiral, the over-work.

02 · The moment before

The cue. What you felt in the thirty seconds before you reached — the ache, the alarm, the lull.

03 · The promise it makes

What the well quietly offers: peace, worth, rest, safety, being seen. Name the real need it's impersonating.

04 · The aftertaste

How you feel after. A broken cistern leaves you needing it again — sharper, with shame. A gift satisfies and lets you move on.

common denominators

Four threads under all of it

These are the family resemblances — why it reads as one shape.

i · Wrong source

Each well structurally cannot deliver what's sought, yet stays close enough to keep you returning.

ii · The cure is the disease

The attempted solution feeds the problem. Effort entrenches the habit; shame triggers the relapse.

iii · Partial satisfaction

It works just enough to reinforce, never enough to free. The near-miss turns a mistake into a loop.

iv · Closed vs. received

A sealed system where the self is both agent and source — versus something taken from outside.

the spine · old / new

A standard that demands, a source that supplies

The Law works like willpower: an external standard that demands performance but supplies no power to produce it. The exit doesn't raise the bar — it moves the source inside.

old · law · works

The closed loop

  • The standard sits outside you, carved in stone — it shows the target, hands you no power.
  • Order: perform → then maybe you're worthy. Worth is the prize you never quite reach.
  • The prohibition can arouse the craving — "do not covet," and coveting wakes up.
  • The bar moves; you re-earn tomorrow. The treadmill is the broken cistern.
new · spirit · grace

The open system

  • The law is written on the heart; stone swapped for flesh, the Spirit put within.
  • Order flips: worth given first → behaviour flows from it. Indicative before imperative.
  • "No condemnation" pulls the shame fuel out of the loop.
  • Change comes by beholding, not clenching — you become what you gaze at.

So effort isn't abolished — it's relocated. Under works, effort tries to generate worth. Under grace, effort expresses a worth already given. Same sweat, opposite engine.

This contrast is sharpest in Protestant readings; Catholic, Orthodox, and Wesleyan traditions integrate works and participation differently. The full treatment — Romans 7–8, Ezekiel 36, 2 Corinthians 3:18 — arrives in the content pass.

two evils, two moves

Stopping isn't the same as returning

Jeremiah names two failures, not one — so the cure has two halves. Do only the first and the vacuum refills on its own. Jesus' picture of exactly this: a spirit leaves, the house is found swept and empty, and it returns with seven worse. Emptiness isn't freedom; it's a vacancy.

move one · necessary, not sufficient

Stop digging

Remove the counterfeit — willpower, deleting the app, white-knuckling. Real, but it only empties the vessel. An empty vessel is an invitation, not a resolution.

move two · the half that holds

Return to the source

Bring the actual need back to a well that fills it. Without this, the loop has nowhere to go but back — the "almost" still calls. Replace, don't just remove.

guardrails

Where this lens breaks

A frame that explains everything predicts nothing. This one earns its keep only where the loop-signature is actually present — and it does real damage when forced onto things it doesn't fit.

not every habit is a broken cistern

A morning walk, a standing call with a friend — neutral or good loops. The frame is about counterfeit-sourced compulsion, not behaviour as such.

sometimes you're just tired, not idolatrous

Scrolling can simply be rest; a snack can simply be hunger. Over-spiritualising ordinary limits is its own trap — scrupulosity — and it manufactures the very shame the gospel was meant to remove.

effort isn't the enemy

Grace produces work. The cistern is effort-as-source-of-worth, not effort itself. This is not a charter for passivity.

grace is not a rival to ordinary means

"Re-source it" never means pray instead of sleeping, instead of therapy, instead of medication, instead of accountability. Those are often how the fountain reaches you.

Field test: does the thing leave you needing it again — sharper, with shame? Or does it actually satisfy and let you move on? Only the first is a broken cistern. The second is just a good gift.

love & its counterfeits

Love is a verb — and so are its fakes

Love is not a passive feeling but an active choice. When we mistake emotional investment for love, we build counterfeits where another person is used to fill our own deficits.

"Love is the will to extend one's self for the purpose of nurturing one's own or another's spiritual growth… It is as love does."synthesised from M. Scott Peck & bell hooks
family I · noun errors

Feelings mistaken for love

Emotional intensities, biological charges, or attachment systems that mimic love but run on automatic nervous-system responses rather than willing action.

family II · false-verb errors

Acts mistaken for love & care

Extractive behaviours where we perform care for another, but the underlying drive is to relieve our own internal scarcity or anxiety. Each one runs the same five-beat loop — and each has a true turn.

the turn

Abundance before the offer

Every counterfeit tries to extract from a person what is missing in ourselves — safety, worth, peace. We hand a human the weight only a transcendent source was meant to carry, crushing both. Love can't be willed out of starvation. Be filled at the source first — and the other is released from being your saviour, free to be received as a gift.
